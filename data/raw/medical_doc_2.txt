Hypertension Management
Hypertension (high blood pressure) is a common condition where the force of blood against artery walls is consistently too high. Blood pressure readings above 130/80 mmHg are considered elevated.

Symptoms:
- Often asymptomatic (silent killer)
- Headaches in severe cases
- Vision problems
- Chest pain

Complications:
- Heart disease
- Stroke
- Kidney disease
- Vision loss

Management:
- DASH diet (low sodium)
- Regular physical activity
- ACE inhibitors or ARBs
- Calcium channel blockers
- Regular blood pressure monitoring
