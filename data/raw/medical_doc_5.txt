Kidney Disease Overview
Chronic kidney disease (CKD) is a progressive loss of kidney function over time. Early stages often have no symptoms.

Symptoms (advanced stages):
- Fatigue and weakness
- Swelling (edema) in legs, ankles, feet
- Nausea
- Decreased appetite
- Changes in urination frequency

Causes:
- Diabetes (most common)
- Hypertension
- Glomerulonephritis
- Polycystic kidney disease

Management:
- Blood pressure control
- Diabetes management
- Dietary restrictions (protein, sodium, potassium)
- Dialysis in advanced stages
- Kidney transplantation
