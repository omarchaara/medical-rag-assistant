Diabetes Mellitus Type 2
Diabetes mellitus type 2 is a metabolic disorder characterized by high blood sugar, insulin resistance, and relative insulin deficiency. Common symptoms include increased thirst, frequent urination, unexplained weight loss, and fatigue.

Risk factors:
- Obesity (BMI > 30)
- Age > 45 years
- Family history of diabetes
- Physical inactivity
- Certain ethnicities (African American, Hispanic, Native American)

Diagnosis:
- Fasting blood glucose ≥ 126 mg/dL
- HbA1c ≥ 6.5%
- Random blood glucose ≥ 200 mg/dL

Treatment:
- Metformin as first-line medication
- Lifestyle modifications (diet, exercise)
- Monitoring blood glucose levels
- Regular follow-up with healthcare provider
